Human Firmware
Update Required

What about the human? What about our stack?

By Branko Lukic · March 2026

Listen to Article
0:00 / 0:00

There is no shortage of AI tools. A Niagara of them. Every day, every hour, every second, a new one lands in your feed. But here is a question we are not asking loud enough: what about the human? What about our stack? Our firmware and software? When do we update that?

The nature of work has shifted. The era of swiping and clicking on screens is fading. The work has lifted into the cognitive arena, working with AI at the level of thought, and that demands something different. Not a new tool. Clarity of the mind.

Imagine a piece of paper scrunched into a tight ball. That is what a busy mind looks like. Eyes open, phone grabbed, sending the mind out. Stimulus after stimulus, rarely pausing and looking inward, into the inner world of ourselves. Most of the day can pass on autopilot, completely detached from self, without even noticing.

Now picture that ball slowly opening. Still crumpled, still messy, but loosening. Next to it, a slightly more unwrinkled version. Then another, smoother still. And all the way to the right, a completely clean, flat sheet of paper.

That clean sheet is clarity. Like sunlight hitting a crisp white page. No wrinkles, no creases, just brightness. A mind in that state knows where it is, knows what it wants to do next. Everything feels available.

And there is good reason for that. The mind is extraordinary. It can hold vast worlds inside it. Vivid imagination, language, spatial awareness, intuition, all running at once. The capacity is already there. But when the mind is pulled in a thousand directions, when it is restless and unsettled, all that richness just churns. It might feel like it is working. But unsettled is not the same as active.

What if there was something that could help with the firmware. Something that does not require a subscription, no download, no waitlist. Something that has been with us all along, available anytime, anywhere, the moment we decide to use it.

There is. It is meditation.

Physical exercise is essential. The body gets healthier, stronger, and the paper gets unwrinkled a little. But the mind is a different system. Ten miles of running, and it can still be restless, still bouncing. The body is fit, but the paper is still crumpled. The mind needs something else entirely — not more movement, not more force, but the opposite.

Meditation does something different. It makes the mind stable. It gathers the energy that gets scattered outward all day and brings it back in.

Think of the mind as river water. Every task, every notification, every exciting moment, even the good ones, stirs up mud. The more activity, the more streams, the murkier the water gets. Sitting down, closing the eyes, quieting everything — the mud settles. The water clears. And upon opening the eyes again, things appear deeper. Dimensions that could not be detected before. Layers in problems that were invisible five minutes ago.

No alcohol needed. No drugs. No external stimulus. This is just how human beings function. Mind and body are connected. That connection simply gets forgotten.

The reason meditation is connected to AI is simple. AI is pushing the work upward, away from the screen, away from the hands, into the cognitive layers. The work now lives in the mind. In critical thinking before critical doing. And without that clarity, without real, deep intent, the output is just noise.

This is the part that gets missed. When genuine stillness is reached, something counterintuitive happens.

Stillness is the ultimate speed.

Not because anything moves faster, but because everything becomes completely clear.

And the most surprising part — the mind does this on its own. There is no forcing the paper flat. Just sitting, closing the eyes, letting go of the stimulus, the jumps, the excitement, the noise of the day. And the paper begins to straighten by itself. The mind knows how to do this. It just needs space.

But it does not stay on its own. Stop meditating, and the paper starts to scrunch back up. The mud returns to the river. The noise fills back in. This is not a one time fix. It is like restarting the system. All that accumulated noise, cleared. It needs to happen regularly.

The next AI tool. The next plugin. The next model release. Everyone is chasing. But the most important upgrade available right now is not on any app store. It is sitting inside the mind, waiting to be uncrumpled.

And this is not about becoming better, stronger, faster. 10x. 100x. Everyone is under that pressure. Scale this. Optimize that. But what exactly is being scaled?

Meditation is not a performance hack. It is a return. A return to mind, body, clarity. Something already there.

And it is free. All of it. Free.

I practice meditation. And even a small pause changes what comes next. Before sitting down at the computer, before opening Claude, or GPT, or Gemini. Even two minutes with closed eyes. Even one minute. Then open the eyes and notice if anything feels different.

It is simple.

Sit down. Close your eyes.
Right here. Right now.
